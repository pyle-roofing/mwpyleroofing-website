Roofers in Pevensey & Pevensey Bay

Pevensey Bay is the most exposed coastal location in our service area — properties here face some of the harshest roofing conditions in the whole of East Sussex. We specify marine-grade materials here and we understand exactly what the coastal environment demands.

Roofing in Pevensey and Pevensey Bay — the most exposed location in East Sussex

Pevensey Bay is as exposed a coastal location as you'll find in East Sussex. Properties on the beach and the seafront road face southwest directly into the prevailing Channel winds with no shelter whatsoever — the sea is immediately outside. Salt spray reaches roofs regularly. Wind loads are at the upper end of what domestic roofing materials are designed to handle. The environment here demands a level of care in material specification and installation quality that simply isn't needed a few miles inland.

Pevensey village itself — with its Norman castle and medieval buildings — is very different in character: a conservation area with listed properties, flint construction, and some of the oldest domestic roofscapes in East Sussex. Westham, between the two, has a mix of older and more recent housing. Each requires a different approach.

The most demanding roofing environment in our service area

Accelerated corrosion of all metal components. At Pevensey Bay, salt air corrosion of roofing fixings happens faster than anywhere else we work. Original iron or galvanised nails, galvanised guttering fixings, and standard steel screws corrode within years rather than decades. We specify stainless steel fixings as standard for all work at Pevensey Bay — it is the only appropriate choice for this location.

Lead flashing failure. The continual thermal cycling from the exposed seafront position — hot sun followed by cold wind and rain — puts particular stress on lead flashings. We inspect and replace lead flashings regularly at Pevensey Bay properties and always ensure correct drip edges and lap dimensions to handle the wind-driven rain that properties here experience.

Ridge tile failure. Mortar-only ridge tile fixing is simply not adequate for Pevensey Bay's wind exposure. We mechanically fix ridge tiles on all Pevensey Bay work — stainless steel fixings through the ridge tile into the batten, in addition to a mortar bed. This is essential at this location.

What materials are right for Pevensey Bay properties?

The importance of marine-grade specification

For Pevensey Bay properties, we always specify with the coastal environment in mind. Stainless steel fixings throughout. Natural Welsh slate or good-quality clay tiles rather than concrete. For guttering and fascia, we specify UPVC systems with stainless steel brackets — cast iron and aluminium both corrode more rapidly than we'd want in this location.

Natural slate — the best choice for seafront exposure

For the older properties at Pevensey Bay and Pevensey village, natural Welsh slate is the most appropriate roofing material and handles the coastal environment better than any alternative. It doesn't absorb water, is unaffected by salt air, and lasts 75–100 years. The fixing nails are the only point of weakness, and stainless steel fixings eliminate that concern entirely.

Clay tiles for non-slate properties

Where concrete tiles are currently installed on Pevensey Bay properties — common on the post-war housing along the seafront — we recommend upgrading to clay at re-roofing time. Concrete tiles absorb moisture and the salt-laden atmosphere at Pevensey Bay accelerates their surface deterioration significantly. Clay tiles don't absorb moisture and perform far better in this environment.

Flat roofs at Pevensey Bay — single ply is the right system

For flat-roofed sections at Pevensey Bay — extensions, garages, bungalow roofs — we recommend single ply membrane over GRP fibreglass, and we strongly advise against felt. In this environment:

  • Felt deteriorates rapidly in UV and salt air — lifespan here is likely less than 10 years
  • GRP fibreglass is excellent but can become brittle in very exposed UV environments over time
  • Single ply membrane systems — particularly EPDM rubber and TPO systems — handle UV exposure, thermal cycling, and wind uplift better than any alternative and come with long manufacturer warranties

We specify the right system for the specific roof and location, and we're happy to discuss the options in detail during a free inspection.

Chimney work at Pevensey and Pevensey Bay

Pevensey village is a conservation area with listed buildings, and chimney work here requires both care and, for listed properties, appropriate consent. We work with traditional materials and mortar profiles to match original construction on the historic properties in Pevensey village.

At Pevensey Bay, the exposed chimney stacks on seafront properties are particularly vulnerable — mortar fails faster in the salt air and freeze-thaw cycles. We inspect and repoint regularly and always recommend addressing mortar joints before they fail entirely and allow water into the stack. For redundant chimneys at Pevensey Bay outside the conservation area, removal is a straightforward option that eliminates a permanent maintenance liability.

Roof cleaning at Pevensey Bay — a different approach

Interestingly, the most exposed seafront properties at Pevensey Bay often have less moss than you might expect — the constant salt spray and wind inhibit biological growth to some degree. However, properties set back slightly from the seafront, and particularly those with any shade from neighbouring buildings, can accumulate moss and lichen. We hand-clean and apply biocidal treatment as for all our areas — jet washing is never appropriate, particularly on the natural slate and clay tiles we specify for Pevensey Bay.

Copper ridge strips work particularly well at Pevensey Bay — the regular rainfall (and occasional sea spray) ensures copper ions are consistently washed down the roof surface, providing ongoing protection against biological growth.

Questions & answers — roofing in Pevensey and Pevensey Bay

My seafront property at Pevensey Bay keeps having ridge tiles blow off — what's the solution?

Mortar-only ridge fixing is not adequate for Pevensey Bay's wind exposure. The long-term solution is mechanically fixed ridge tiles — stainless steel fixings through the tile into the batten — in addition to a mortar bed. This is standard practice for us on all Pevensey Bay ridge work and ends the cycle of repeated call-outs for blown-off ridges.

How quickly do fixings corrode at Pevensey Bay?

Standard galvanised nails can corrode through within 10–15 years in the Pevensey Bay environment — sometimes less on the most exposed seafront properties. Stainless steel fixings are the only appropriate specification here, and we use them as standard on all Pevensey Bay work. If your property has an older roof with original or galvanised fixings, it's worth an inspection to assess their condition.

My property in Pevensey village is listed — what do I need to know about roofing work?

Listed building consent is required for any significant change to the external appearance of a listed building, including roofing materials. Like-for-like repair generally doesn't require consent but it's always best to check with Lewes District Council first. We work regularly on listed properties in the Pevensey conservation area and can advise on what's appropriate and acceptable.

What flat roof system do you recommend for a Pevensey Bay bungalow?

For the most exposed Pevensey Bay locations, we recommend single ply membrane — EPDM or TPO — over GRP fibreglass. Single ply handles UV exposure, thermal cycling and wind uplift better than GRP in the very exposed seafront environment, and comes with long manufacturer warranties. We'd carry out a site inspection before specifying — the right system depends on the specific roof area, falls, and drainage arrangement.
