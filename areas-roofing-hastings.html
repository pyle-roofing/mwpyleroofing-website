Roofers in Hastings & St Leonards-on-Sea

Hastings is one of the wettest towns in the southeast of England and has some of the most varied and demanding roofing conditions in East Sussex. We've been working here for decades and know exactly what roofs in this area need.

Roofing in Hastings — what makes this area different

Hastings and St Leonards-on-Sea present a fascinating and genuinely challenging roofing environment. The town receives significantly higher rainfall than most of East Sussex — one of the highest totals in the entire southeast — combined with strong onshore winds from the Channel and a mix of sun and humidity that creates near-perfect conditions for moss, lichen and algae growth on roofs. If you live in Hastings, you'll almost certainly have noticed how green roofs get here compared to inland areas.

The housing stock is equally varied. The Hastings Old Town — net shops, fishermen's cottages, Georgian and early Victorian properties tucked between the cliffs — is a conservation area with significant restrictions on roofing materials. The town centre and the Ridge have substantial Victorian and Edwardian terraces. St Leonards-on-Sea has grand seafront properties, many converted into flats with large flat roof sections. Ore, Hollington and Silverhill have 1930s through to 1980s housing. Each area has its own roofing character and its own set of common problems.

The most common roofing problems in Hastings

Severe moss and algae growth. This is the standout roofing issue in Hastings. The combination of high rainfall, humidity, and the shade created by the cliffs and hills in parts of the town means roofs here can become heavily colonised with moss within just a few years of cleaning. Left untreated, moss does real structural damage — it holds moisture against the tile surface, accelerates freeze-thaw cracking, works beneath tiles and lifts them, and blocks gutters causing overflow and damp.

Lead valley and gutter failures. Hastings Old Town has a significant number of properties with original lead internal gutters and valleys. These can last an extraordinarily long time when properly maintained, but when the lead cracks, lifts or pulls away from its fixings, the resulting leaks are often severe. Lead repairs and replacements in the Old Town are a significant part of our work in Hastings.

Flat roof deterioration on St Leonards conversions. The large Edwardian and Victorian properties along the St Leonards seafront that have been converted into flats very often have substantial flat roof sections — some original, some added during conversion. Many of these are now well past the end of their useful life, and the consequences of failure in a multi-occupancy building can be significant.

Tiles vs slates in Hastings — what's right for your property?

Natural slate for Old Town and conservation properties

In the Hastings Old Town conservation area, natural slate is almost always the required material and is specified by Hastings Borough Council for any re-roofing. Original properties in this area were built with natural slate — Welsh predominantly — and the council requires like-for-like replacement to preserve the historic character of the area. We source appropriate natural slate and are experienced in working within conservation area requirements in Hastings.

Clay plain tiles

Clay tiles were the standard material for much of Hastings' Victorian and Edwardian housing stock, particularly the terraces of the town centre and St Leonards. They have a lifespan of 60+ years, handle the high rainfall in Hastings well, and don't absorb water in the way that concrete tiles do — making them significantly more resistant to the freeze-thaw cracking that's a real issue in a wet climate like Hastings. When re-roofing a period Hastings property, upgrading from concrete back to clay is an investment that pays for itself.

Concrete interlocking tiles

Many 1960s–80s properties in Ore, Hollington and on the Ridge have concrete interlocking tiles, often now reaching the end of their life. They're the most budget-friendly option to replace like-for-like, but in Hastings' wet climate they absorb moisture and can crack over time. If you're re-roofing a Hastings property with concrete tiles, it's worth a conversation about whether clay or synthetic slate might be a better long-term investment.

Flat roofs in Hastings — felt, GRP or single ply?

Why felt is a poor choice in Hastings

In a town with Hastings' rainfall levels, traditional felt flat roofing is particularly ill-suited. The seams and laps in felt systems are the weak points, and the constant saturation that Hastings roofs experience accelerates their failure. We've replaced many felt roofs in Hastings — both on extensions and on the larger flat sections of St Leonards conversion properties — where the felt has failed in under 10 years. We don't recommend felt for new flat roof installations in this area.

GRP fibreglass — the right choice for most Hastings flat roofs

A GRP fibreglass flat roof is seamless — there are no joints or laps for water to find. It handles high rainfall, standing water and UV exposure extremely well, and has a lifespan of 25–30 years. For extensions, bay roof sections, garages and smaller flat roof areas in Hastings and St Leonards, GRP is our standard recommendation. It costs approximately 40–60% more than felt but you're unlikely to need to replace it again within your time in the property.

Single ply for larger roofs

For larger flat roof sections — particularly relevant on the converted seafront properties in St Leonards with significant flat roof areas — single ply membrane systems offer superior performance and manufacturer-backed warranties of 20–25 years. They're the professional standard for commercial flat roofing and increasingly the right choice for large residential flat roofs in a wet coastal environment like Hastings.

Chimney work in Hastings

Hastings has an extraordinary density of chimney stacks, particularly in the Old Town and the Victorian terraces. Many are now purely decorative — no fires have been lit beneath them for generations — but they remain a live maintenance liability. The mortar between bricks expands and contracts with Hastings' frequent wet-dry cycles and eventually fails, allowing water into the stack. The lead flashing where the chimney meets the roof is another common failure point.

Repointing is the most common chimney job we carry out in Hastings — raking out failed mortar and replacing it with fresh, correctly specified mortar is a straightforward job that extends the life of a stack by 20–30 years. It should be done before water gets into the brickwork and causes structural damage.

Chimney rebuilding is needed when repointing is no longer sufficient — when the brickwork has deteriorated to the point where the structure is compromised. We carry out chimney rebuilds regularly in Hastings, working carefully to match the original brick and mortar profile, which is especially important in the Old Town conservation area.

Chimney removal is worth considering for redundant stacks causing repeated problems. In the Old Town conservation area, removal below roof level is generally not permitted, but outside the conservation area it's a clean and permanent solution. We remove the stack, cut off below roof level, make good the rafters, felt and batten, and re-tile — leaving the roof completely weathertight and eliminating a permanent maintenance liability.

Roof cleaning in Hastings — why jet washing damages your roof

Because Hastings roofs get mossy faster than almost anywhere else in East Sussex, roof cleaning is something many homeowners here look into. We want to be very clear about something: jet washing a tiled roof causes serious, permanent damage. The high-pressure water strips the surface texture from concrete tiles, blasts away the protective mineral granules, damages the interlocking edges and can drive water deep beneath tiles. We see the consequences of jet-washed roofs regularly in Hastings — surfaces that were mottled and rough are now smooth and porous, absorbing water rapidly and deteriorating years ahead of schedule.

How we clean roofs in Hastings: We clean by hand using soft brushes to remove the bulk of the moss and growth, then apply a professional-grade biocidal treatment that kills moss, lichen and algae at the root level — including the root structures that remain invisible on the surface but continue feeding. The treatment breaks down safely and doesn't damage plants, gutters or neighbouring surfaces. Results are visible within weeks and continue to improve as dead growth weathers away over several months.

Copper ridge strips — the long-term solution. After cleaning, we strongly recommend fitting copper strips along the ridge of the roof. Every time it rains, tiny amounts of copper ions wash down across the roof surface. Moss, algae and lichen cannot survive in the presence of copper ions. The result is a roof that stays clean for years — rather than months — before any re-treatment is needed. It's a small additional cost at the time of cleaning that pays for itself many times over. We fit copper ridge strips on properties across Hastings and St Leonards and the results are consistently excellent.

How to extend your roof's life and prevent damp in Hastings

  • Keep gutters clear. In Hastings' high-rainfall environment, blocked gutters overflow within minutes during heavy rain. That water runs down your walls, saturates the soil against your foundations, and finds its way into your property. Clear gutters in autumn and again in spring — or twice in autumn if you have trees nearby.
  • Deal with moss before it deals with you. Don't wait until the roof is completely green. A biocidal treatment when moss is first appearing is far cheaper than remedial work after moss has lifted tiles and allowed water in.
  • Check your ridge tiles every few years. Hastings' wet-dry cycles are hard on ridge mortar. A simple visual check from the ground after winter storms — looking for any ridge tiles that look raised, tilted or displaced — can catch a problem before it becomes a leak.
  • Ensure your loft is ventilated. Poor loft ventilation is a major cause of condensation damp in Hastings homes — the warm moist air from the living spaces rises, hits cold roof timbers and deposits moisture. If you're seeing black mould on timber in the loft, this is likely the cause, not a roof leak.
  • Have flashings checked after storms. The lead or mortar flashings around chimneys, dormer windows and where a lean-to meets a wall are the most vulnerable points on a Hastings roof. A few minutes of checking these areas after any significant storm is good practice.

Questions & answers — roofing in Hastings

My roof is covered in moss — should I be worried?

Yes, but it's a manageable problem. Heavy moss growth holds moisture against the tile surface, works into joints and under tile edges, and blocks gutters. It will shorten your roof's life if left untreated. The solution is professional hand cleaning with a biocidal treatment — not jet washing, which causes its own damage. We'd recommend adding copper strips to the ridge at the same time to prevent rapid regrowth. Call us for a free assessment.

I'm in the Old Town conservation area — can I choose my own roofing materials?

In the Hastings Old Town conservation area, the council generally requires like-for-like replacement using natural slate. Synthetic alternatives are often not approved. We're experienced at working within these constraints and can guide you through what's permitted for your specific property.

My flat roof leaks every winter — is a repair enough or do I need a new roof?

If your flat roof leaks every winter, it's almost certainly at or past the end of its useful life. Repeated patching of a failing felt flat roof costs more over time than a proper replacement, and a new GRP fibreglass roof will give you 25–30 years without the annual anxiety. We'll inspect it honestly and tell you which situation you're in.

How often should a Hastings roof be professionally cleaned?

Without copper ridge strips, moss typically returns within 2–4 years in Hastings' wet climate, and cleaning is needed every 3–5 years. With copper ridge strips fitted after cleaning, most roofs in Hastings stay clean for 7–10 years or more. The copper strips pay for themselves in saved cleaning costs within the first cycle.

I can see daylight through my loft — is this an emergency?

It depends on where the light is coming from. Small amounts of light around ridge tiles or at the very edges (eaves) can be normal ventilation. A clear gap where a tile or slate is missing is not normal and should be addressed promptly — water will get in during the next rain. Call us and we'll come out quickly.
