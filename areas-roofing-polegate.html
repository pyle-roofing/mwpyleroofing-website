Roofers in Polegate & Willingdon

Polegate and Willingdon sit just a few miles from our Eastbourne base, which means we're among the first on site when roofing problems arise here. We know this area well — both the older village properties and the significant modern housing that now makes up most of the town.

Roofing in Polegate and Willingdon — position and exposure

Polegate and Willingdon occupy an interesting position in the East Sussex landscape — close enough to the coast to receive some salt air influence, but set back enough and slightly elevated enough to also experience significant South Downs wind exposure. The gap in the Downs at Willingdon creates a natural wind funnel, and properties on the eastern and northern edges of Polegate and across Willingdon can experience quite different wind conditions to Eastbourne just a few miles south.

The housing in Polegate ranges from Victorian and Edwardian properties in the original village centre — some with original clay tile or slate roofs — through significant 1930s and post-war suburban development, to the large modern estates that have expanded the town considerably since the 1980s. Stone Cross has a slightly different character again, with a mix of older farmhouse and cottage-style properties alongside more recent development.

Common roofing issues in Polegate and Willingdon

Wind-lifted ridge tiles. The South Downs wind gap exposure means ridge tile mortar in Polegate and particularly Willingdon takes more punishment than in more sheltered locations. We see more wind-lifted ridge tiles per square mile in this area than in central Eastbourne. Mechanical fixing of ridge tiles — using stainless steel screws rather than mortar alone — is something we increasingly recommend for exposed Polegate and Willingdon properties.

Modern concrete tile deterioration. The large estates built in Polegate and Stone Cross from the 1980s onwards are now entering the period where their concrete interlocking tiles need attention — crazing, loss of surface coating, and cracking are all appearing on properties built in the late 1980s and 1990s. These estates collectively represent a significant volume of re-roofing work over the next decade.

Felt flat roofs on extensions. The 1950s–80s semi-detached housing throughout Polegate's residential streets has a high proportion of felt flat-roofed rear extensions now well past their useful life. GRP replacement is our standard recommendation.

Tiles, slates and materials for Polegate properties

For modern estate housing

Concrete interlocking tiles for like-for-like replacement on modern Polegate estates are the most budget-friendly option. However, given the wind exposure in parts of Polegate and Willingdon, we recommend ensuring tiles are well-nailed with stainless steel fixings rather than relying on the interlocking mechanism alone. For properties where the roof is being completely re-done, it's worth discussing whether clay tiles — more durable and better-looking — represent better long-term value.

For older village properties

The Victorian and Edwardian properties in Polegate village centre and the older parts of Willingdon should be re-roofed in clay or natural slate as appropriate to their original specification. These properties often have good original tiles that can be re-laid — strip, replace damaged tiles, re-nail with stainless steel, and re-lay is often a more cost-effective and better-outcome solution than replacing all tiles.

Flat roofs — GRP is the right choice for Polegate

Given the wind exposure in parts of Polegate and Willingdon, the seamless nature of GRP fibreglass flat roofing is particularly relevant — there are no seams to catch the wind and lift. A GRP flat roof is bonded directly to the deck and presents a continuous, unbroken surface that handles wind loading well. We recommend GRP for all flat roof replacements in Polegate and Willingdon.

Chimney work in Polegate

Polegate's older properties — the Victorian terraces and cottages of the original village — have chimney stacks that are now showing their age. Repointing is the most common job, addressing mortar joints that have failed in the Wealden weather. For wind-exposed properties in Willingdon, chimney stacks can be a particular structural concern — a leaning or unstable stack on an exposed ridge needs professional assessment promptly.

For the many redundant chimneys on Polegate's post-war and modern housing, full removal is a clean, cost-effective solution that we carry out regularly. Polegate has no blanket conservation area restrictions on chimney removal, making this a straightforward option where wanted.

Roof cleaning in Polegate — hand cleaning and copper strips

Polegate and Willingdon see reasonable moss growth — less severe than Hastings but more than the most exposed coastal sites where salt air inhibits establishment. The modern estates in particular develop moss steadily on north-facing slopes, and properties near hedgerows and trees grow it faster.

We clean roofs in Polegate by hand — soft brush removal of growth, followed by biocidal treatment. We never jet wash — it permanently damages concrete tiles, which make up the majority of Polegate's modern housing stock. After cleaning, copper ridge strips provide lasting protection against moss regrowth, typically extending the period before re-treatment is needed from 3–4 years to 8–10.

Questions & answers — roofing in Polegate and Willingdon

My ridge tiles keep coming loose — why does this keep happening?

In Polegate and Willingdon's exposed position near the Downs wind gap, mortar-only ridge tile fixing is often not sufficient for the long term. Wind gets under lifted tiles and accelerates failure. We recommend mechanically fixing ridge tiles with stainless steel screws in addition to a mortar bed — this significantly reduces the likelihood of repeat problems on exposed Polegate and Willingdon properties.

How long will a new flat roof last on my Polegate extension?

GRP fibreglass: 25–30 years. Single ply: 40+ years. Traditional felt: 10–15 years. Given how rarely you want to deal with a flat roof replacement, we always recommend GRP as the minimum for Polegate extensions. The additional cost over felt is recovered in avoided repeat replacements well within 20 years.

My 1990s house tiles look grey and patchy — do they need replacing?

Grey, patchy colouring on 1990s concrete tiles usually indicates the surface coating is wearing away and the tile is beginning to absorb water. This is a sign that the tiles are approaching the end of their useful life, but doesn't necessarily mean immediate replacement — a biocidal clean and inspection will tell us more. We'll give you an honest assessment of how long they have left.
