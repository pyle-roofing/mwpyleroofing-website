Roofers in Bexhill-on-Sea

Bexhill-on-Sea is one of the most distinctively Edwardian towns in East Sussex, and that character is written into its rooflines — clay plain tiles, decorative terracotta ridges, ornate chimney stacks and original cast iron guttering. We love working here and know its roofs well.

Roofing in Bexhill-on-Sea — what makes this area different

Bexhill-on-Sea was largely built between 1890 and 1930, which gives it an unusually consistent Edwardian and early 20th century housing stock. The characteristic roofing material is clay plain tiling — the small, flat tiles with a gentle camber that give Bexhill its warm, handmade roofline character. Many properties also have decorative ridge tiles, finials, and original clay hip tiles. This is the roofing tradition we were trained in and work with every day.

Being a seafront town, Bexhill faces significant salt air corrosion — particularly for properties west of the De La Warr Pavilion where the prevailing wind comes directly off the sea. The original fixing nails on Edwardian clay-tiled roofs in Bexhill are almost universally now at or past the end of their life, and widespread nail sickness — slates or tiles slipping because the nails holding them have rusted through — is extremely common on the older properties closest to the seafront.

The most common roofing problems we see in Bexhill

Nail-sick clay tiles on Edwardian properties. The majority of Bexhill's original Edwardian clay-tiled roofs were fixed with iron nails that are now corroded through. This manifests as tiles slipping steadily down the roof — often accelerating after a storm. The tiles themselves can be perfectly sound (clay tiles last 60–100 years), but once nail sickness is widespread, a full strip and re-lay with stainless steel fixings is the only lasting solution.

Mortar haunch failures on decorative ridges. Bexhill's Edwardian properties often have beautiful decorative ridge tiles — ogee profiles, crested ridges, ornate hip tiles. The mortar haunching that holds these is now failing on most original roofs, and finding the right replacement profile when individual ridge tiles need replacing requires specialist knowledge and sourcing. We take care to match original profiles where possible.

Cast iron guttering failures. Many Bexhill properties still have original cast iron guttering — it looks wonderful but requires maintenance. Joints fail, sections rust through, and the painted finish needs periodic attention. We repair and replace cast iron guttering, and can advise on modern alternatives that replicate the period profile.

Clay tiles vs concrete tiles in Bexhill — why the original material is worth restoring

Clay plain tiles are the correct and original material for Bexhill's Edwardian housing stock, and they're genuinely superior to concrete alternatives for this area. A good quality clay plain tile has a lifespan of 60–100 years, handles the Bexhill seafront's salt air exposure significantly better than concrete, and — unlike concrete — doesn't absorb moisture, meaning it's resistant to the freeze-thaw cracking that's accelerated in exposed coastal locations.

We frequently encounter Bexhill properties where a previous owner replaced original clay tiles with cheaper concrete alternatives. The concrete tiles often don't fit the original batten spacing correctly, age poorly in the coastal environment, and look wrong on a period property. When we're re-roofing in Bexhill, we always recommend returning to clay where budget allows — and source appropriate tiles from quality suppliers.

Flat roofs in Bexhill — felt, GRP or single ply?

Many Bexhill properties have flat-roofed rear extensions or garages — typically added in the 1960s–80s with traditional felt. Most of these are now well past their lifespan.

GRP fibreglass is our standard recommendation for flat roof replacements in Bexhill. Seamless, durable (25–30 year lifespan), completely waterproof and low-maintenance — it's simply the most sensible upgrade from felt for extensions and garages. The Art Deco flat-roofed sections on some of Bexhill's interwar properties also benefit significantly from GRP or single-ply replacement; these roofs deal with pooling water and need a system that handles standing water without joints or seams failing.

Chimney work in Bexhill

Bexhill's Edwardian chimney stacks are substantial structures — often four or five pots, decorative clay capping, and several metres of brickwork above the roof line. They're a defining visual feature of the town but a significant maintenance responsibility.

Lead flashing renewal is the most common chimney job we do in Bexhill. The step and soaker flashings where the chimney meets the roof, and the back gutter behind the chimney, have a lifespan of 50–80 years for lead but often much less for the mortar pointing over the lead. We renew flashings regularly across Bexhill, usually catching them before a leak develops.

Pot replacement and capping — cracked chimney pots are common on Bexhill properties after cold winters. We replace pots like-for-like and, for redundant flues, fit purpose-made ventilated caps that keep weather out while allowing the flue to breathe.

Stack repointing — Bexhill's salt air and wet winters are hard on chimney mortar. We repoint stacks before water penetration causes brickwork to spall (break apart) — once the face of the brick is damaged, the stack needs rebuilding rather than repointing, which is a significantly larger job.

Roof cleaning in Bexhill — hand cleaning, biocidal treatment and copper strips

Bexhill roofs — particularly north-facing slopes and properties shaded by mature trees in the residential streets behind the seafront — collect moss and lichen steadily. Salt-laden air also encourages certain types of algae growth that can give tiles a dark, streaked appearance.

Why we never jet wash roofs in Bexhill. Jet washing a clay or concrete tiled roof causes permanent damage. On clay plain tiles specifically — Bexhill's most common roofing material — the high-pressure water strips the weathered surface, damages tile edges, and dislodges the granular texture that gives the tile its grip. We have seen jet-washed Bexhill roofs where tiles that were structurally sound before cleaning are now porous, smooth and deteriorating rapidly. It is not a cleaning method, it is a form of damage.

Our approach is to clean by hand using soft brushes to remove moss and growth from the surface, then apply a professional biocidal treatment. The biocide penetrates and kills moss, algae and lichen at root level — including the invisible root structures that remain after surface removal. The dead material weathers away naturally over the following months, and the tile surface is left clean, undamaged and treated.

Copper ridge strips are the finishing touch we recommend for every Bexhill roof we clean. Copper ions washed down by rainfall create an environment hostile to moss and algae — they simply cannot establish on a copper-treated surface. On a property in Bexhill that might otherwise need cleaning every three to four years, copper strips extend that interval to eight to ten years. It's a small investment with a significant long-term return.

How to protect your Bexhill roof and prevent damp

  • Don't let moss accumulate. Moss on clay tiles acts as a sponge — it holds water against the tile surface, adds weight, and works beneath tile laps. Treat it early with a biocidal solution rather than waiting until the roof looks completely green.
  • Keep cast iron gutters painted. Unpainted cast iron rusts from the inside out. A coat of black bitumen paint on the inside of cast iron gutters every five years keeps them serviceable almost indefinitely.
  • Check the mortar on your ridge after winter. Bexhill's seafront winds are hard on exposed ridge mortar. A quick visual check from the ground after winter — looking for any ridge tiles that appear raised, tilted or separated from the tiles below — is good annual practice.
  • Ensure loft ventilation is adequate. Condensation in the loft is frequently misidentified as a roof leak. Warm moist air from the living space rises into a cold loft and condenses on the timbers — creating damp patches and even mould on roof timbers that has nothing to do with the roof covering. Adequate cross-ventilation at eaves level prevents this.

Questions & answers — roofing in Bexhill-on-Sea

My Edwardian property has original clay tiles — should I replace them or restore?

If the tiles themselves are sound — not cracked, not crumbling — it's almost always worth restoring rather than replacing. Strip the roof, replace any damaged tiles with matching clay, re-nail with stainless steel fixings, and re-lay. You preserve the original character, avoid sending tonnes of good clay tiles to landfill, and end up with a roof that should last another 40–50 years. We inspect and advise honestly on how many tiles are salvageable.

Can I get matching clay tiles if some need replacing?

Usually yes, though it sometimes takes careful sourcing. For widespread replacement, new clay plain tiles in an appropriate profile are available from several UK manufacturers. For individual tile matching on a conservation property, reclaimed tiles are sometimes the better option. We source both and will find the closest available match for your roof.

How long will a GRP flat roof last on my Bexhill extension?

A properly installed GRP fibreglass flat roof has a lifespan of 25–30 years and requires very little maintenance during that time. Compare this to a felt flat roof — 10–15 years at best in Bexhill's coastal environment — and the additional upfront cost of GRP represents excellent value over the roof's lifetime.

Is it worth having my chimney removed in Bexhill?

If the chimney is genuinely redundant and causing repeated maintenance issues — failing mortar, recurring flashing leaks, cracked pots — then removal is worth serious consideration outside the conservation area. We remove the stack, make good the roof structure and tile over, leaving the roof completely weathertight. It eliminates a permanent maintenance liability for the life of the building.
