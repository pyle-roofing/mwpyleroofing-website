Roofers in Brighton & Hove

Brighton and Hove has some of the most architecturally spectacular rooflines in England — Regency parapets, mansard roofs, lead valleys on Georgian terraces — alongside everything from Victorian villas to modern apartment blocks. We bring the full range of skills needed to work here.

Roofing in Brighton & Hove — a city of roofing extremes

Brighton and Hove presents a wider variety of roofing challenges than almost anywhere else in East Sussex. At one end of the scale, the Grade I listed Regency terraces of Brunswick and Kemp Town have complex parapet gutters, original lead roofing details, and ornate chimneys that require specialist knowledge and painstaking care. At the other, the post-war and modern housing of Moulsecoomb, Whitehawk and Portslade has straightforward concrete tile roofs needing like-for-like maintenance. Between these extremes, the Victorian villas of Preston Park and Seven Dials, the Edwardian semis of Hove, and the converted mansion blocks along the seafront each have their own roofing character.

The coastal exposure in Brighton and Hove is significant — the city faces southwest directly into the prevailing Channel winds, and properties along the seafront, on the raised ground of Kemp Town, and on the exposed western edge of Hove face very high wind loads and salt air corrosion. We specify materials accordingly.

The most common roofing challenges in Brighton & Hove

Parapet gutter failures on Regency properties. The grand Regency terraces of Brunswick, Palmeira, and Kemp Town are built with hidden parapet gutters — internal lead-lined channels behind the parapet walls that collect rainfall and channel it to downpipes. When these fail, water can flood into roof voids and upper floors. Parapet gutter relining in lead or single ply is specialist work that we carry out carefully and correctly.

Flat roofs on mansion block conversions. Brighton and Hove has an enormous stock of large Victorian and Edwardian properties converted into flats, very often with substantial flat roof sections on upper floors. These are frequently on felt at or past the end of their life. In a multi-occupancy building, a flat roof failure isn't just an inconvenience — it can affect multiple properties and creates real urgency. We carry out flat roof replacements on Brighton mansion blocks efficiently and to a high standard.

Victorian slate nail sickness. The Victorian terraces of Preston Park, Seven Dials, Hanover and the North Laine area have original Welsh slate roofs, most of which are now experiencing nail sickness — the original iron fixing nails have corroded and slates are slipping. This is very widespread across Brighton's Victorian stock and we carry out strip and re-lay work regularly in these areas.

Tiles vs slates in Brighton & Hove — what's right for your property?

Natural Welsh slate — for Victorian and conservation properties

For Brighton's Victorian terraces, natural Welsh slate is almost always the correct and most appropriate material. Many streets in Hanover, Preston Park, Seven Dials and Kemp Town fall within conservation areas and the council expects like-for-like slate replacement. The slate itself lasts 75–100 years — the issue is always the fixings, which we replace with stainless steel on re-lays.

Clay plain tiles for Edwardian Hove

The substantial Edwardian semis and terraces of Hove, Portslade and the streets between New Church Road and the seafront were typically roofed in clay plain tiles. These should be restored like-for-like where possible — clay tiles of this era can still be sound after 100 years and a re-nail and re-lay is often the appropriate solution rather than full replacement.

GRP and single ply for flat roofs

For Brighton's flat roof sections — mansion block tops, rear extensions, bay roofs — GRP fibreglass is our standard recommendation for areas up to around 40 square metres. For larger areas, particularly the significant flat roof sections on larger converted properties, single ply membrane with a manufacturer warranty is the professional standard. We would never recommend felt for a Brighton seafront property — the UV exposure, salt air and wind loading will shorten its life significantly.

Chimney work in Brighton & Hove

Brighton's Victorian and Regency chimney stacks are part of the city's visual character, and many conservation area designations specifically protect them. Removal within conservation areas requires planning consent and is unlikely to be approved on prominent rooflines.

The most common chimney work we do in Brighton is lead flashing renewal — the step and soaker flashings on Victorian chimney stacks need renewing approximately every 50 years, and many Brighton stacks are now on their second set of flashings or beyond. We also carry out repointing on weathered stacks, pot replacement on cracked or missing pots, and chimney rebuilding where the brickwork has deteriorated beyond repointing.

For genuinely redundant chimneys on properties outside conservation areas — particularly in the post-war and 1960s–80s housing of Moulsecoomb, Whitehawk and east Brighton — full removal is a clean and cost-effective long-term solution that we carry out regularly.

Roof cleaning in Brighton & Hove — never jet wash, always hand clean

Brighton's seafront location and relatively mild, damp winters mean moss, algae and lichen growth is common on roofs — particularly on north-facing slopes and properties in the sheltered streets between the Downs and the seafront. We see a great deal of jet-washed roofs in Brighton that have been damaged by the process — concrete tiles with their surface stripped, slate with its surface texture blasted away, even clay tiles with cracked edges from the pressure.

We clean by hand only — soft brushes to remove the bulk of the growth, followed by a professional-grade biocidal treatment. The biocide kills moss, algae and lichen at the root and the dead material weathers away over the following months. Tiles are left undamaged and treated.

Copper ridge strips are particularly effective in Brighton's mild coastal climate. The copper ions washed down by rainfall prevent moss re-establishing on treated tiles — extending the interval between cleans from 3–4 years to 8–10 years. We fit copper strips as standard on every Brighton roof we clean.

Preventing damp in Brighton & Hove homes

  • Parapet gutters need regular inspection. On Regency and Georgian properties, the hidden parapet gutter is the highest-risk element of the entire building. Have it inspected every 5 years — catching a small failure before it floods is far cheaper than remedial work after water has damaged the roof structure and upper rooms.
  • Keep valley gutters clear. Brighton's Victorian properties often have lead valleys that collect leaves and debris. A blocked valley fills with water during heavy rain and overflows into the roof void. Clear in autumn.
  • Check flashings after storms. Brighton's exposed southwest-facing position means wind-driven rain tests flashings hard. Check around chimneys, dormer windows and abutments after any significant storm.
  • Ventilate the loft. Condensation in loft spaces is frequently confused with roof leaks in Brighton's older housing stock. Adequate cross-ventilation at eaves level prevents moisture build-up on roof timbers.

Questions & answers — roofing in Brighton & Hove

I'm in a Brighton conservation area — what materials can I use for re-roofing?

Brighton and Hove City Council generally requires like-for-like replacement in conservation areas — natural slate where slate is existing, clay tiles where clay is existing. Upgrading from concrete to clay is usually acceptable; changing from slate to concrete is not. We can advise specifically for your property and street.

My Regency terrace has a parapet gutter that's leaking — how urgent is this?

Very urgent. A failing parapet gutter in Brighton can quickly flood a roof void and cause serious damage to upper floor ceilings, plasterwork and timbers. Call us promptly on 01323 767955 and we will prioritise an inspection.

Is GRP suitable for a large flat roof on a Brighton mansion block?

For sections up to around 40 square metres, GRP is excellent. For larger areas — multi-flat roof sections or entire top-floor flat roofs on large conversion properties — single ply membrane systems are generally more appropriate, offering better performance at scale and manufacturer-backed warranties. We'll assess your specific roof and recommend the right system.

How long does roof cleaning last in Brighton?

Without copper ridge strips, moss typically re-establishes within 3–5 years in Brighton's mild coastal climate. With copper ridge strips fitted after cleaning, most Brighton roofs stay significantly cleaner for 8–10 years. We always recommend copper strips — the long-term saving in cleaning costs is substantial.
