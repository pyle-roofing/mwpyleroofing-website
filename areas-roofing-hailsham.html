Roofers in Hailsham

Hailsham is one of the fastest-growing towns in East Sussex and one of our busiest regular service areas. From older cottages and Victorian terraces in the town centre to the large modern estates that have expanded the town significantly, we cover every property type here.

Roofing in Hailsham — what this area needs

Hailsham sits in the Wealden district inland from the coast, which means it doesn't face the same salt air challenges as Eastbourne, Bexhill or Hastings — but it makes up for that with high Wealden rainfall and a clay-heavy soil environment that creates specific damp challenges for older properties. The town has grown rapidly over the past 30 years and now has a very wide mix of housing — from the Victorian and Edwardian properties of the town centre and the lanes of Old Hailsham to substantial modern housing estates on the eastern and southern edges.

Being inland, Hailsham roofs grow moss differently to coastal properties — less wind-accelerated, more steady biological growth in a damp, relatively still environment. North-facing slopes and any properties near trees or hedges accumulate moss steadily. Without treatment, Hailsham roofs can become significantly mossy within 5–7 years.

Most common roofing problems in Hailsham

Ageing concrete interlocking tiles on modern estates. Hailsham's extensive 1980s–2000s housing stock is largely roofed in concrete interlocking tiles. These have a lifespan of 25–30 years, which means a large proportion of Hailsham's modern housing is now entering the period where tiles need attention — cracking, crazing, and loss of surface coating are all common. Many of these roofs need either significant repair or full re-roofing within the next 5–10 years.

Felt flat roofs on extensions. Hailsham's older housing in the town centre and the suburban streets built between the 1950s and 1980s very commonly has felt flat roofs on rear extensions that are now at or past their end of life. GRP replacement is the right answer for virtually all of these.

Guttering capacity on modern housing. Hailsham's higher rainfall means guttering capacity matters. Many of the modern housing developments were built with standard guttering that, while adequate in average conditions, overflows during heavy Wealden downpours. Overflowing gutters cause damp to external walls, saturate the soil against foundations, and eventually penetrate into the building.

Tiles vs slates for Hailsham properties

Concrete interlocking tiles — and when to upgrade

For modern Hailsham properties originally built with concrete interlocking tiles, like-for-like replacement is the most budget-friendly option. However, with Hailsham's reasonable rainfall and no salt air to contend with, clay tiles are an excellent upgrade option at re-roofing time — longer lifespan (60+ years vs 25–30), better moisture resistance, and a significantly smarter appearance. The additional cost at re-roofing time is modest over the life of the roof.

Natural and synthetic slate for older properties

Victorian and Edwardian properties in Hailsham town centre were originally slated. Where these are being re-roofed, natural Welsh slate is the correct material for listed or conservation properties. For non-designated properties, high-quality synthetic slate (40–50 year lifespan) is an excellent and more affordable alternative that looks the part on a period Hailsham property.

Flat roofs in Hailsham — GRP is the clear recommendation

In Hailsham's wet Wealden climate, the seamless nature of GRP fibreglass flat roofing is particularly valuable. There are no joints to fail under repeated saturation. A GRP flat roof on a Hailsham extension has a lifespan of 25–30 years and requires virtually no maintenance during that time. The comparison with felt — 10–15 years, multiple repair cycles, annual anxiety — makes GRP the straightforward choice for any flat roof replacement in Hailsham.

Chimney work in Hailsham

Hailsham's older housing has chimneys in various states of repair. The Victorian terraces in the town centre have original stacks that now need repointing — the Wealden clay has limited ability to absorb very wet winters without mortar suffering. We regularly repoint chimney stacks in Hailsham's older streets before water penetration causes more serious brickwork damage.

For genuinely redundant chimneys on Hailsham properties — and there are many, particularly on the post-war housing where gas heating has long since replaced open fires — full chimney removal is a popular and sensible option. We remove the stack, make good the roof structure and tiling, and the result is a cleaner roofline with no future maintenance liability. Hailsham doesn't have the same conservation area restrictions that make this difficult in Lewes or Hastings Old Town, so removal is generally straightforward to arrange.

Roof cleaning in Hailsham — hand cleaning, biocide and copper strips

Hailsham's inland position and relatively still air means moss grows steadily and persistently — particularly on north and east-facing slopes. We see roofs here that have become significantly colonised within just a few years, especially on properties near the mature hedgerows and trees on the edges of older Hailsham estates.

Jet washing damages roofs — we never use it. High-pressure water strips the surface coating from concrete tiles (extremely common on Hailsham's modern housing), blasts away protective granules, and drives water under tile laps. The short-term visual result of a jet-washed roof — it looks clean immediately — conceals the damage done. Within a year or two, the stripped tiles are absorbing water far more readily and moss regrows faster than it did before. We hand-clean with soft brushes and apply a professional biocidal treatment.

Copper ridge strips for Hailsham properties are particularly good value in a moss-prone inland environment. The steady Wealden rainfall means copper ions are washed consistently down the roof surface — creating conditions hostile to moss establishment. Roofs we've cleaned and fitted copper strips to in the Hailsham area are remaining clean for significantly longer than untreated roofs. It's one of the best-value additions to a roof cleaning job.

Preventing damp in Hailsham homes

  • Check gutters twice a year. Hailsham's autumn brings heavy leaf fall and significant rainfall in quick succession — exactly the conditions for gutters to block and overflow. Clear in late October and again in March.
  • Address moss before it blocks gutters. Heavy moss slides off roofs during storms and blocks gutters and downpipes rapidly. Keeping the roof treated prevents this.
  • Watch for concrete tile crazing. On Hailsham's modern housing, fine surface crazing on concrete tiles is a sign that the tiles are absorbing water and approaching the end of their life. Water absorbed into crazed tiles then freezes in cold winters and accelerates the breakdown. Address early.
  • Ensure extension roofs drain freely. Flat roof extensions need their outlets checked and cleared regularly — a blocked outlet means standing water, which accelerates failure of any roof covering.

Questions & answers — roofing in Hailsham

My 1990s Hailsham house has concrete tiles that are cracking — do I need a full re-roof?

Possibly. Cracking and crazing on concrete tiles from the late 1980s and 1990s is very common in Hailsham and usually indicates the tiles are at or near the end of their life. We'll inspect and tell you honestly whether individual replacements will suffice or whether a full re-roof makes more sense economically. Often at this age, the felt beneath the tiles is also deteriorating and a full re-roof is the better long-term solution.

Is GRP suitable for a small garage roof in Hailsham?

Absolutely — GRP fibreglass is ideal for garage flat roofs. It's seamless, durable (25–30 years), can be walked on, and looks smart. It's significantly more expensive than felt upfront but you won't be replacing it again within your time in the property.

How do I know if my chimney needs repointing or rebuilding?

Repointing is appropriate when the brickwork itself is sound but the mortar between bricks has cracked, eroded or fallen out. Rebuilding is needed when the brickwork has deteriorated — bricks are spalling (face breaking away), the stack is leaning, or there is significant structural deterioration. We can inspect and advise. Catching a repointing job before it becomes a rebuild is always much better value.
